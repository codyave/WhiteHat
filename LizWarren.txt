
Sep 1970 → Begins teaching at a public school for kids with disabilities

Jun 1971 → Is about five or six months pregnant with her first child
         → Leaves her job at the public school
         → In a 2007 interview with Berkeley University, she says:
             - she was teaching on an emergency certificate
             - she left because she lacked necessary teaching credentials
             - she went to graduate school that summer for add'l education courses, but questioned whether that is what she wanted
             - she gave birth to her first child at the end of summer
             - she stayed home for a few years, but got restless

Dec 1972 → At a Christmas party in Oklahoma, old friends from the high school debate team suggested she enroll in law school

Sep 1973 → Enrolls in Rutgers Law School at Rutgers University–Newark in New Jersey

Jun 1976 → Is about nine months pregnant with her 2nd child, Alexander
         → Graduates from Rutgers; passes the bar exam; earns her law degreee
         → In a 2011 interview with Rutgers University, she says:
              - at the time, employers weren't looking to hire a pregnant woman
              - old friends from Oklahoma were the ones who had suggested she enroll in law school
              - her law degree had helped her overcome the difficulty in finding a job while pregnant ("hung out a shingle")

Sep 2010 → Nominated by Obama as Special Advisor to Treasury Secretary on the Consumer Financial Protection Bureau

Nov 2012 → Defeats Massachusetts GOP candidate Scott Brown in a race for an open Senate seat

Apr 2014 → In "A Fighting Chance", Liz says she considered graduate school for speech pathology, but decided on law

Oct 2019 → At a rally in Nevada, Liz says she wasn't invited back by the principal at the end of the school year in 1971
